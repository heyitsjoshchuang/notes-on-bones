Resource Compilation

2022-08-15
2022-08-17
2分鐘閱讀

This post is a on-going compilation of resources, including tutorials, performances, and other information on bones.

First of all, when it comes to online resources on bones, I have to put Rhythm Bones Central on the top of the list. Run by the rhythm bones society, it gives you a comprehensive tour on this ancient percussion instrument, with information from the historical background of the bones, to how to make, play, and find places and conventions to perform in.

The following are more resources on bones, split into categories.

Introductions

Resources that gives an overview of the bones.

  • How to Play Bones with Dom Flemons (YouTube) Dom Flemons explains how one may approach a playing style that combines two-handed bones playing, the harmonica and foot tapping.
  • BONES with Brad Dutz - Part 1 of 3 (YouTube) The first in 3 introductory videos by Brad Dutz, covering the the playing of the bones, as well as how the materials, contruction, and the player’s grip affects the tone output.
  • Playing the Bones with Boris (YouTube) Frantic yet clean playing style by a calm man, Boris Sichon.

Tutorials

Resources in the format of a tutorial.

  • Hans Plays Bones (YouTube) Hans Weehuizen teaching the basics of bones playing, featuring slow motion footage of the roll.
  • Bone Rhythm Ep 2 How to Hold the Bones (YouTube) The first in 6 (Ep 2 to Ep 7) introductory videos by Graham Hargrove, demontrating how to play a variety of rhythms on the bones.
  • Kafari rhythm Bones tutorials (YouTube) A playlist of 3 tutorials by Kafari, demontrating a relaxed playing style.
  • Irish Rhythm Bones Lesson by James Yoshizawa (YouTube) James Yoshizawa demonstrating 2 excercises on two-handed bones playing, said to have been adapted from other percussion rudiments.
  • Rhythm Bones Technical Rudiments Tutorial 1 (YouTube) The first in 8 videos on exploring technical rudiments in detail by the channel Beyond Belief.

Performances

Resources that are not explicitly created as lessons, but still great for learning.

  • Rhythm Bones Solo on Percuss.io (YouTube) An example of written-out bones rhythms, played by Mark Shelton.
  • Steve Brown (bones) and Raymond Henry (flute) Performing in Tubbercurry, Co. Sligo 11/18/10 Steve Brown playing a matched pair in a reel and a jig, accompanying a flute.

Miscellaneous

Resources that provide information on aspects of the bones other than playing.

  • The Sound of Rhythm Bones by Michael Baxter (YouTube) A catalog of bones timbres.
