Grip

2022-08-23
2022-08-23
2分鐘閱讀

The bones can be held in the hand in various ways. For the purpose of this wiki, only one is covered.

Description

Slot one bone between the base section (proximal phalanx) of the index and middle fingers, and the other between the middle and ring fingers. The two bones should curve away from each other. Curve the fingers at the base and middle joint as if you are making a fist, and the middle and ring fingers lay on the side of the bones.

Mechanics

The bone between the index and middle fingers is pinned to the palm with your middle finger. It should not move with respect to the palm, hence it is referred to as the fixed bone. The other one is allowed to pivot around the middle and ring finger, hence the free bone.

In The Rudiments of Rhythm Bones1, the terms stationary and moving are used.

Timbre

The pivot point of the free bone has a great impact on the timbre of the bones. When the free bone extends from the top of the hand around 1/3 of its length, the bones makes a dull, less piercing sound. When it’s held closer to the end i.e. less of the bone is extending out, they make a bright, more piercing sound.

If one end of the bones is thicker than the other, holding the thinner end and having the thicker end below makes a thicker sound. Conversely, having the thinner end below makes a thinner sound.

Movement

The greater portion of the free bone is below the pivot formed by the fingers, the more inertia it has. Therefore it requires more force to be moved at the same speed, and produces a louder sound.
